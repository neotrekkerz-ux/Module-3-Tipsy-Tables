Tipsy Tables

Stability simulation — drag a block, watch the physics

Four tables. Four shapes. Same surface area. Same four legs — placed at the centroid of an equilateral triangle inscribed in each shape. That means the legs are not centered for most shapes.

A uniform block of mass m can slide anywhere on the surface. Your task: rank the four tables from most stable (hardest to tip) to least stable, before running the simulation.

Rank — most stable (1) to least stable (4)

Drag the block anywhere on the tabletop. A leg turns red when its normal force reaches zero — it has lifted.
